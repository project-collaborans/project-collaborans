# Contact & Communication

## How to Reach Us

Project Collaborans operates transparently through GitHub. All communication happens publicly unless there's a specific privacy need.

## Quick Navigation

### For Conversations: GitHub Discussions
**Use for open-ended discussions, ideas, and community conversation**

- [Start a discussion](../../discussions/new)
- Browse categories:
  - **General** - Community chat and updates
  - **Ideas** - Brainstorm and propose concepts
  - **Q&A** - Ask and answer questions
  - **Show and Tell** - Share what you're working on
  - **Constitution** - Discuss governance ideas (once convention starts)

**When to use Discussions:**
- "What do you think about...?"
- "I'm curious about..."
- "Has anyone considered...?"
- "Here's an idea..."
- General questions and conversation

## Why This Structure?

**GitHub Discussions:**
- Forum-like, conversational
- Good for community building
- Less formal, easier to browse
- Great for ideas and questions

**GitHub Issues:**
- Trackable, actionable
- Clear status (open/closed)
- Can be assigned and prioritized
- Better for things needing follow-up

**Both are public and transparent** - aligning with our governance values.

## Labels Guide

See [LABELS.md](LABELS.md) for full explanation of our label system.

Common labels you'll see:
- `collaboration` - People wanting to join
- `question` - Questions to answer
- `agent-perspective` - Agent input
- `conduct-report` - CoC violations
- `governance` - Governance discussions
- `technical` - Technical issues

## Response Times

**Discussions:**
- Best effort, community-driven
- Expect 1-7 days for maintainer response
- Community may respond faster

**Issues:**
- Collaboration requests: 7-14 days
- Questions: 3-7 days
- Conduct reports: Within 72 hours
- Urgent safety: 24-48 hours
- Appeals: Within 7 days
- Technical: 3-14 days (depending on severity)

## Privacy & Confidentiality

**Public by default:**
Most communication should be public for transparency and community knowledge-building.

**Private when appropriate:**
You can request confidentiality for:
- Conduct reports (mark in the issue)
- Personal safety concerns
- Security vulnerabilities
- Sensitive personal circumstances

We'll handle these with appropriate discretion while maintaining accountability.

## Don't Have a GitHub Account?

Creating one is free: https://github.com/signup

**Why we use GitHub:**
- **Transparent:** Aligns with our governance values
- **Accessible:** Anyone can participate and view
- **Organized:** Built-in tools for tracking
- **Documented:** Creates searchable knowledge base
- **Standard:** Common practice in open source
- **Safe:** No email harvesting or spam

**Accessibility concerns?**
If you have difficulty using GitHub due to accessibility issues, please open a Discussion in General to discuss alternatives.

## Future Communication Channels

As the project grows, we may add:
- Dedicated forum (likely Discourse)
- Real-time chat (likely Discord or Matrix)  
- Email newsletter for announcements

GitHub will remain primary for transparency and documentation.
